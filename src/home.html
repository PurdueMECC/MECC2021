Harnessing the Power of Waves

About

The Batchelors are a team of highly interdisciplinary students at Purdue University participating in the Marine Energy Collegiate Competition. Our goal is to develop a novel coupling between a Wave Energy Converter and Batch Reverse Osmosis system that reduces the Levelized Cost of Water for desalination.

We believe seawater desalination is a powerful solution to mitigate water insecurity. We are supported by Purdue University School of Mechanical Engineering, the Warsinger Water Lab and the National Renewable Energy Laboratory.

Wave energy

We live on a water planet. Roughly 71 percent of the Earth is covered in the stuff, and it’s constantly in motion -- tides surging rhythmically; powerful currents coursing beneath the surface; waves rolling across oceans and crashing against continents.

Around 1.2 billion people, or almost one-fifth of the world’s population, live in areas of water scarcity, mostly in developing countries. Traditional reverse osmosis (R/O) desalination systems offer a solution but require sufficient electrical grid capacity. Ocean waves provide a consistent, reliable source of clean energy. Harnessing this energy to power desalination systems will increase resilience in coastal communities, particularly in those with less developed infrastructure relying on expensive imported fuel to power desalination plants.
