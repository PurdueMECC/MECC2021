Timeline

  • Project Description:

    Our design, Osmocean, is a coupling system between a wave energy converter (WEC) and a batch reverse osmosis (BRO) system. A WEC will drive the pressurization of water for BRO via this coupling. Osmocean will achieve increased efficiency and reduced levelized cost of water as compared to existing desalination systems. BRO reduces the energy consumption of desalination by allowing applied pressure to build over time. Effectively integrating this process with time-variant wave energy via a novel mechanical coupling will usher more affordable, sustainable desalination systems into coastal markets.

  • Game Plan:

    Through the second week of February, we will be iterating through our design, creating a low-fidelity prototype, and increasing our competency in hydrodynamic modeling with help from the Warsinger Water Lab. By the first week of March at the latest, our purchase orders will be submitted for all parts, after which, we will have a mid-fidelity prototype completed. During this time, we will also be working on modeling our system. Over the month of March, we will complete a high-fidelity prototype so that we can validate and test the design during the first week and a half of April. We began our business plan at the beginning of February with the assistance of a student from Purdue’s Krannert School of Management. We have also been working on an environmental analysis of our design which will be under consistent review throughout the project.

  • Team Strengths:

    • Resources between the Warsinger Water Lab at Purdue University.
    • Strong highly variable skillset of the mechanical engineers on our team – aviation, CAD, modeling/simulation, controls, fluid dynamics, thermodynamics, design, automation, heat transfer, environmental engineering.
    • Graduate student and faculty advisors along with business and environmental engineering experts.

  • Team Hurdles:

    • Diversity - Most team members are majoring in Mechanical Engineering, but we do have team members with backgrounds in entrepreneurship, Spanish, ECE, Environmental Engineering, German, management, and aviation.
    • Understanding the batch reverse osmosis system previously developed in the Warsinger Water Lab.
    • Time constraints - It will be challenging to fit MECC in with senior design requests from us all in one semester, but this could also be considered a strength. With our MECC and senior design projects being one and the same, we do not have to split our time between them.
    • COVID - Restrictions make it more difficult to both meet in person and access facilities that would normally be more accommodating.

  • Competition Objectives:

    We are excited to gain prototyping and testing experience and welcome the challenges presented by a constrained budget and tight deadline. We look forward to presenting our results and networking with industry representatives and academic scholars at ICOE in April. Above all, we are aiming to create a functional product that is adaptable to different WEC archetypes so that desalination can be easily implemented around the world.
